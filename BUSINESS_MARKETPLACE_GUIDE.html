Both sides of the Pet10x services marketplace are now live and wired to the database: a business owner publishes a priced service catalog and runs bookings through to payment, and a pet owner discovers that business, books a service, and reviews it afterwards. This guide explains what was built and gives you a click-by-click script to test the whole loop.

2 · The Booking Loop

Every state below is a real row transition in service_bookings, guarded by row-level security. Nothing is faked.

stateDiagram-v2 [*] --> requested: pet owner books a service requested --> confirmed: owner accepts requested --> declined: owner declines (with reason) confirmed --> in_progress: owner starts the job confirmed --> cancelled: either side cancels requested --> cancelled: pet owner cancels in_progress --> completed: owner marks done completed --> paid: owner records payment
(writes payments row, 15% commission) completed --> [*]: pet owner can now review paid --> [*] declined --> [*] cancelled --> [*]
Who can do what. The pet owner can create a booking and cancel their own. The business owner can accept, decline, start, complete and record payment — but only on bookings for their own business. A review can only be written by the customer of a booking that actually reached completed or paid. All of this is enforced in the database, not just the UI.

3 · Test the Whole Loop

About ten minutes. Keep both windows open side by side — refresh the other side after each step to watch the state move.

Part A — Pet owner books

  1. Sign in at /app as sofia.nguyen@pet10x.com / Password123! and open the Services tab. Three verified businesses, each with a rating, an “Open/Closed” badge derived from real opening hours, and a distance if your location is set.
  2. Tap “Happy Paws Grooming”. A storefront with the description, tags, a priced service list (Full Groom $85, Bath & Brush $45, Nail Trim $20, De-shedding $70, Puppy's First Groom $60), the weekly hours table with today in bold, and real customer reviews including the owner's reply.
  3. Tap “Book” on Nail Trim. Pick the pet Biscuit, choose a date/time, add a note like “nervous with clippers”, and hit Request. A green confirmation. The booking is now a real requested row — you are not charged.
  4. Open “My bookings” (button in the top-right of the Services screen). Your new booking at the top marked Requested — “Waiting for the business to confirm” — with a Cancel button.

Part B — Business owner runs the job

  1. In the other browser, sign in at /businessaccess as businessowner@pet10x.com / 12345678. The Dashboard shows a “Verified & listed” banner, a New requests tile that has gone up by one, and your request at the top of “Needs you now” with an SLA countdown.
  2. Open the Bookings tab. It defaults to Requests. Sofia's request, showing the pet (Biscuit, Golden Retriever), her note, and the money split — $20.00 gross, −$3.00 fee, $17.00 net — visible before you accept.
  3. Press Accept. Then follow it through: Start job → Mark complete → Mark paid. The card moves Requests → Upcoming → In progress → History, changing status each time. “Mark paid” writes a real payment row and splits the commission.
  4. Open the Earnings tab. Gross collected, platform fee and Net to you all increased by this booking, and it appears in the per-booking ledger. “Export CSV” downloads a real spreadsheet.

Part C — Pet owner reviews

  1. Back in the pet owner window, reload My bookings. The booking now reads Paid, and a “Leave a review” button has appeared (it only appears once a booking is completed or paid).
  2. Leave a review — pick a star rating, write a comment, post it. The row now shows “You rated N/5”. Go back to the Happy Paws storefront: your review is listed, and the business's headline rating has recalculated automatically.
  3. In the business window, open the Reviews tab and reply to your new review. Your reply appears under the review — and the pet owner can see it on the public storefront.

Part D — Prove the guardrails (optional but satisfying)

  1. Storefront tab → edit anything (description, tags, hours, add a new service) and save. The “What customers see” preview updates live, and your changes appear on the pet owner's storefront after a refresh. A new service is immediately bookable.
  2. Try to un-verify yourself. There is deliberately no UI switch for it — verification is admin-only, and the database rejects the write even if you call the API directly. An error: “Only Pet10x admins can change verification status.” A business can never mint its own trust badge.
  3. Promotions tab → New campaign → sponsor a building. Before you buy, the reach is shown as aggregate counts only — “N pet owners, N dogs, N cats”. There is no way to see who those residents are.
One thing that is intentionally simulated: “Mark paid” records the payment in-app (a real payments row with the commission split) rather than charging a card — this project has no Stripe keys. Everything else in the loop is a genuine database transition. Wiring Stripe Connect for real payouts is a separate integration; the ledger is already the source of truth for what is owed.

4 · The Business Owner Portal

Six tabs at /businessaccess. Every button writes to the database — there are no placeholder actions.

📊 Dashboard

  • Visibility banner — verified, or a completeness meter that names the exact next blocker.
  • KPI row — new requests, jobs today, booked in 30 days, rating.
  • Needs you now — unanswered requests with an SLA clock, completed jobs awaiting payment, reviews without a reply.
  • Today's schedule — the day sheet.

📅 Bookings

  • Segmented inbox — Requests · Upcoming · In progress · History, with live counts.
  • Net-of-fee preview — gross, commission and net shown before accepting.
  • Full lifecycle — accept, decline with a reason, start, complete, record payment.
  • Customer + pet card — name, pet, breed and the note they chose to share. Nothing more.

🏪 Storefront

  • Live preview of the exact card customers see, updating as you type.
  • Profile — name, category, description, price range, tags, location + GPS, service radius.
  • Opening hours — per day; these drive the “Open now” badge automatically.
  • Service catalog — add/edit/hide/delete priced services. This is what residents book.

📣 Promotions

  • Building-targeted campaigns — sponsor a specific building's community feed.
  • Aggregate reach before purchase — pet owners, dogs, cats. Never a resident list.
  • Directory featured / banner with a radius, plus a run-length window.
  • Pause / resume / delete any campaign.

💰 Earnings

  • Gross · platform fee · net — no surprises, 15% commission shown explicitly.
  • Pipeline — value of confirmed and completed work not yet paid.
  • Per-booking ledger with date, customer, service, and the split.
  • CSV export for bookkeeping.

⭐ Reviews

  • Every review with stars, author and date.
  • Reply publicly — replies show on the storefront.
  • Awaiting reply counter to nudge reputation management.
  • Ratings are computed from real reviews, not a static number.

7 · Hard Boundaries

A business sees its own commercial surface and aggregate market data — never a person it hasn't been introduced to by a booking. All of this is enforced by row-level security and verified by an automated test.

BoundaryHow it's enforcedVerified
Cannot self-verify (mint the trust badge)A database trigger rejects any change to is_verified by a non-admin, even via the API✅ returns “Only Pet10x admins can change verification status”
Cannot read resident rosters or unit listsNo policy grants a business access to resident_links✅ returns 0 rows
Cannot read a pet it has no booking forPet access is scoped to pets on that business's own bookings✅ returns 0 rows
Can see the customer + pet on its own bookingNarrow policies keyed to “has a booking with this person/pet”✅ name, pet and breed all resolve
Cannot touch another business's bookingsUpdate policy requires owner_id = auth.uid() on the parent business✅
Campaign targeting shows counts, not peopleA dedicated function returns only aggregate totals per building✅
A review requires a real completed bookingInsert policy checks the booking belongs to the author and reached completed/paid✅
Fixed along the way: the business owner could previously have flipped their own is_verified flag to true and appeared in the directory without review — the trust badge was self-serve. That is now blocked at the database level.

8 · What Changed Under the Hood

Four migrations, all applied to the live project and checked into supabase/migrations/.

ChangeWhy it was needed
New business_services tableThere was no way to publish what a business offers or what it costs. Bookings now reference a real catalog item.
Booking lifecycle — status became a proper enum; added service, pet, note, decline reason and response timestampStatus was free text with no states, so there was no lifecycle to run and no way to measure response time.
Booking write policies critical fixservice_bookings had select and insert policies but no update policy at all — a business owner accepting or completing a booking would have silently done nothing. This is what made the inbox actually work.
business_mark_booking_paid functionRecording payment also writes a payments row, which is service-role-only, so it runs through one authorised function that verifies ownership and splits the commission.
New business_reviews table + rating triggerrating_avg / rating_count were aggregates with nothing behind them. Ratings are now recomputed from real reviews on every insert, update and delete.
Verification guardClosed the self-verification hole described above.
Booking-scoped visibilityA business could not see the customer's name or the booked pet at all. Now it can — for its own bookings only.
Aggregate reach functionLets a business size a building's audience for campaign targeting without exposing any resident identity.
